Belief in Elarion: The Philosophy of Essence

The One Shared Truth

Across Elarion, nearly everyone agrees on one fundamental fact: An Essence permeates all things - living beings, stone, air, magic, even time itself.

  • Magic is not a miracle - it is the shaping of Essence. No one agrees on what that means.
  • The Verdant is not evil, good, or divine - it is Essence made willful.
  • Dragons may or may not exist - if they do, they may simply understand Essence better than anyone else.
  • Everything else is interpretation.

Essence as Philosophy, Not Faith

Beliefs in Elarion answer questions, not prayers:

  • Should Essence be shaped?
  • Should it be restrained?
  • Is the Verdant a correction or a catastrophe?
  • Is progress defiance or destiny?
  • Does Essence care?

There are regional schools of thought, not universal religions.

Major Philosophical Currents

These are worldviews, not churches. They overlap, fracture, and contradict each other.

1. The Essence as a Tool

"A river exists to be crossed."

Belief: Essence exists to be shaped, refined, and improved upon. Magic is labor. Progress is proof.

Common Among: Humans (especially artificers and builders), Forged, War-focused cultures.

View of the Verdant: A hostile phenomenon - a mistake, an enemy, or a natural disaster to be overcome.

Role in Play: Justifies risky magic, encourages innovation, creates escalation with nature.

2. The Essence as a Balance

"Push too hard, and the world pushes back."

Belief: Essence flows in cycles. Shaping it too forcefully causes backlash - the Verdant is evidence.

Common Among: Elves, Half-orcs, Some halfling tribes, Rural survivors.

View of the Verdant: Not evil - a response.

Role in Play: Encourages restraint, supports rituals and traditions, explains why resurrection is rare and dangerous.

3. The Essence as Indifferent

"The river does not know your name."

Belief: Essence has no will, no judgment, no morality. Magic works. Consequences happen. Thatâ€™s all.

Common Among: Scholars, Some dwarves, Gnomes (quietly), Cynics and veterans.

View of the Verdant: Neither punishment nor protector - just a phenomenon.

Role in Play: Rationalizes brutal choices, encourages exploration and study, often clashes with moral arguments.

4. The Essence as Sacred

"You don't carve your name into a living thing."

Belief: Essence deserves reverence, not exploitation. Magic is a violation unless done with permission or humility.

Common Among: Verdant sympathizers, Orcs, Forest elves, Isolated communities.

View of the Verdant: A guardian, a purifier, or even a last defense.

Role in Play: Generates ideological conflict, creates sanctified zones, explains hostility to floating cities.

5. The Essence as a Lie

"The world wants us weak."

Belief: Essence is a trap - a system that enforces limits. Only by dominating it completely can mortals be free.

Common Among: Bastion Noctis war-mages, The Lich and his followers, Extremists and radicals.

View of the Verdant: A jailer.

Role in Play: Antagonist philosophy, explains dark magic and necromancy, justifies atrocities.
